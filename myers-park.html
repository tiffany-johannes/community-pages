The Neighborhood

A Century of Charlotte's Finest

Designed by legendary landscape architect John Nolen in the early 1900s, Myers Park was Charlotte's first planned suburb. Today, its willow-oak canopy, grand boulevards, and stately homes represent the highest expression of Queen City living. This isn't just a neighborhood — it's an institution.

Freedom Park

The crown jewel: 98 acres with a 7-acre lake, walking trails, tennis courts, playgrounds, and an amphitheater. The annual Festival in the Park has drawn crowds since 1964. Mornings here feel like a painting.

Fine Dining & Culture

The Fig Tree restaurant sits in a historic home. Stagioni brings upscale Italian to Selwyn Avenue. The Mint Museum Randolph is steps away. Charlotte Country Club and Myers Park Country Club anchor the social scene.

SouthPark & Shopping

SouthPark Mall — anchored by Nordstrom and Neiman Marcus — is adjacent to Myers Park. Morrison Boulevard adds boutiques, high-end home goods, and upscale dining minutes from your front door.

Architectural Heritage

Tudor revivals, Georgian colonials, renovated bungalows, and new luxury builds share these streets. Many lots exceed half an acre. The architecture tells the story of Charlotte's last 100 years.

Elite Schools

Selwyn Elementary, Alexander Graham Middle, and Myers Park High (IB programme) represent strong public options. Providence Day, Charlotte Latin, and Charlotte Country Day are among the Southeast's top private schools.

Minutes from Everything

Uptown Charlotte is a 10-minute drive. The Little Sugar Creek Greenway connects you by foot and bike. Queens University adds collegiate energy. It's a rare blend of serenity and centrality.

Common Questions

Myers Park Real Estate FAQ

The median sold price in Myers Park is $1,345,000 per RPR (Realtors Property Resource), with an estimated home value of $1,764,820 and a median list price of $2,274,145. Luxury estates regularly exceed $2M to $5M+.
Public: Selwyn Elementary, Alexander Graham Middle, and Myers Park High School (with its International Baccalaureate programme). Private: Providence Day School, Charlotte Latin School, Charlotte Country Day School, and Christ the King Catholic School — collectively among the Southeast's finest.
Myers Park is approximately 3–4 miles from Uptown Charlotte, about a 10–15 minute drive. The Little Sugar Creek Greenway also provides a walking and biking connection to the city center.
Myers Park spans primarily 28207 (core Myers Park) and 28209 (southern Myers Park / SouthPark border). The western edge overlaps with 28203 (Dilworth area).
Myers Park is one of Charlotte's most desirable neighborhoods. It offers an unmatched concentration of top schools — public (Selwyn Elementary, Myers Park High IB) and elite private (Providence Day, Charlotte Latin, Charlotte Country Day). Freedom Park provides 98 acres of green space, and the tree-canopied streets are safe and walkable. The community is deeply invested in education and quality of life.
Myers Park is in Mecklenburg County with a property tax rate of approximately 1.05% of assessed value. Given the high home values (median $1,345,000), annual property taxes typically range from $10,000 to $25,000+ depending on the property. Mecklenburg County reassessments can significantly impact tax bills in appreciating areas like Myers Park.
Myers Park offers historic character, tree-canopied boulevards, proximity to Uptown (10 min), and elite private schools, but at a significantly higher price point (median $1,345,000 vs $631,000). Ballantyne offers newer construction, more modern amenities, and a suburban town center at a lower entry point. Myers Park is for buyers seeking prestige, history, and centrality; Ballantyne for those wanting modern suburban living with strong schools.
The most prestigious addresses include Queens Road West, Cherokee Road, Hermitage Road, and Colville Road — featuring grand estates on large lots. The Freedom Park area commands top dollar for park-adjacent living. Biltmore Drive and Brandon Road offer stately homes with mature landscaping. For newer luxury construction, look for teardown-to-custom-build opportunities throughout the neighborhood.
Myers Park is approximately 15–20 minutes from Charlotte Douglas International Airport (CLT) via Woodlawn Road to Billy Graham Parkway, covering about 10 miles. The central location makes airport access convenient without the long suburban commute.
Myers Park is a premium neighborhood with a median sold price of $1,345,000. Property taxes at Mecklenburg's ~1.05% rate are substantial. Many properties have no HOA or low HOA fees ($0–$200/month) since most homes are on individual lots, not in managed communities. Private school tuition ($15K–$30K+/year) is an additional consideration for many Myers Park residents.
