The Neighborhood

What Makes Ballantyne Special

Located 18 miles south of Uptown Charlotte off I-485, Ballantyne has evolved from quiet farmland into one of the region's most dynamic live-work-play communities. Think manicured streetscapes, a buzzing town center, nationally ranked schools, and a growing roster of restaurants and entertainment venues that make leaving the neighborhood entirely optional.

The Bowl & The Amp

Ballantyne's crown jewel. Stream Park, curated dining, and The Amp concert venue bring national acts and weekend energy to the heart of the neighborhood. It's the outdoor living room of South Charlotte.

Dining & Culture

From Gallery Restaurant's seasonal American plates to Tabla's Indian cuisine and Crispy Banh Mi's Vietnamese street food, the culinary scene punches well above its suburban weight class.

Golf & Green Space

The Golf Club at Ballantyne offers a championship 18-hole course. Hawk Ridge Nature Preserve adds 117 acres of trails and bird-watching. Greenways thread through every neighborhood pocket.

Shopping & Retail

Ballantyne Village, Blakeney, and Stonecrest put groceries, boutiques, and weekend errands within minutes. National retailers sit alongside locally owned shops and cafes.

Top-Rated Schools

Ballantyne Elementary, Community House Middle, and Ardrey Kell High School consistently rank among Charlotte-Mecklenburg's best. Residents move here for the schools and stay for everything else.

Access & Commute

I-485 connects you to SouthPark, Uptown, and Charlotte Douglas Airport without breaking a sweat. Close enough to the city to work there, far enough to leave it behind at the end of the day.
