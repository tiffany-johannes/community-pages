The Community

Where Small-Town Meets Exceptional

Twenty-five miles south of Charlotte's glass towers, Waxhaw and Marvin offer something the city can't: a genuine Main Street, Friday night football under the lights, weekend farmers markets, and neighbors who actually know your name. But don't mistake quiet for sleepy — this area is one of the fastest-growing and highest-performing real estate markets in the Charlotte metro.

Historic Downtown Waxhaw

Known as the "Antique Capital of the Carolinas," downtown Waxhaw's tree-lined streets are filled with locally owned boutiques, art galleries, and restaurants. First Fridays, the fall festival, and the Christmas parade keep the community calendar full year-round.

Dining & Breweries

From Dreamchaser's Brewery to Provisions' farm-to-table plates and Salty Parrot's Caribbean vibes, the local food scene has grown into a genuine draw. Plus, Blakeney and Waverly shopping centers are just 15 minutes north.

Parks & Outdoor Living

Cane Creek Park spans 1,050 acres with a lake, camping, and trails. The Twelve Mile Creek Greenway connects neighborhoods on foot and by bike. Anne Springs Close Greenway in nearby Fort Mill adds 2,100 more acres of trails.

Best-in-Class Schools

Union County Public Schools is consistently rated A+ statewide. Marvin Ridge High School ranks among North Carolina's top public high schools. Cuthbertson High rounds out the options with strong academics and athletics.

Space to Spread Out

Unlike Charlotte's denser suburbs, Waxhaw and Marvin offer larger lots, mature trees, and rolling terrain. Master-planned communities like Cureton and Providence Downs sit alongside estate properties on acreage.

Easy Charlotte Access

Highway 16 and I-485 put Uptown Charlotte 30–35 minutes away. Charlotte Douglas Airport is under 40 minutes. Close enough for the career, far enough for the lifestyle.

Common Questions

Waxhaw & Marvin Real Estate FAQ

The median sold price in Waxhaw-Marvin (28173) is $690,000 as of February 2026 per RPR (Realtors Property Resource). The median list price is $699,000 and the estimated home value is $732,050. Prices range widely from townhomes in the $400Ks to estate homes over $1M.
Union County Public Schools serves both communities and is consistently rated A+ by Niche. Key schools include Marvin Ridge Elementary, Marvin Ridge Middle, Marvin Ridge High School (top 10 in NC), and Cuthbertson High School. Several excellent private options like Providence Day School are also nearby.
Waxhaw is approximately 25 miles south of Uptown Charlotte — about a 30–35 minute drive via Highway 16 or I-485. Charlotte Douglas International Airport is roughly 35–40 minutes away.
Both share the 28173 zip code and Union County schools, but they differ in character. Waxhaw is a historic town with a vibrant downtown, diverse housing options, and a growing population (~20,000+). Marvin is a smaller, more exclusive village (~7,000 residents) known for large-lot estates and a semi-rural feel, with a median home price roughly double that of Waxhaw.
Waxhaw and Marvin have been among the most resilient and consistently appreciating markets in the Charlotte metro. The combination of top-rated schools, limited land for new development, and strong demand from relocating buyers supports long-term value. The area continues to attract buyers from the Northeast and Midwest seeking quality of life and relative affordability.
