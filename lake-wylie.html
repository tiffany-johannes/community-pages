The Lifestyle

Wake Up to the Water

Lake Wylie stretches across 13,400 acres of water just southwest of Charlotte, straddling the North Carolina-South Carolina border. The South Carolina side — including Clover and the Lake Wylie community — offers waterfront living, lower taxes, excellent schools, and a pace of life that revolves around the dock, the sunset, and the simple joy of being on the water.

Life on the Lake

Boating, paddleboarding, kayaking, fishing, and swimming — all from your backyard or one of several community marinas. Wide coves, updated docks, and year-round water access make this the daily lifestyle, not just a weekend escape.

Clover, SC

The town of Clover brings small-town Southern charm to the Lake Wylie area. A revitalizing Main Street, community festivals, and Clover School District (one of York County's best) make it a favorite for those seeking both lake proximity and community roots.

SC Tax Advantages

South Carolina offers lower property tax rates, no state tax on Social Security, and a homestead exemption. For many homeowners, living on the SC side of Lake Wylie means thousands in annual tax savings compared to Mecklenburg County.

Parks & Nature

McDowell Nature Preserve offers 1,100+ acres of trails, camping, and lake access on the NC side. Ebenezer Park on the SC side provides boat ramps, picnic areas, and shoreline relaxation. Nature is never more than a few minutes away.

Dining & Waterfront Fun

Dock-and-dine restaurants, lakeside bars, and growing retail options serve the Lake Wylie community. For bigger dining and entertainment, Charlotte's South End and Uptown are a short drive north.

Charlotte in 25 Minutes

Lake Wylie feels like a world away, but Uptown Charlotte is roughly 25 minutes via I-77 and I-485. Charlotte Douglas Airport is about 20 minutes. The best of both worlds: lake life with city access.
